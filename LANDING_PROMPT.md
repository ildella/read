# Landing Page Prompt — Frankie Read

Build a landing page for **Frankie Read**, a calm reading-and-listening app for web articles. The product is a modern successor to Pocket: save articles from your browser, read them clean, or listen to them with beautiful Text-to-Speech.

---

## Product context

- **Tagline**: "Turn the web into your personal podcast."
- **Secondary tagline**: "Save articles. Listen to them. Finally finish what you started reading."
- **Audience**: Knowledge workers, developers, founders, researchers, avid readers — people who save articles but never get to them. Former Pocket users. People who walk, commute, or cook and want to consume long-form content in those moments.
- **Tone**: Calm, confident, editorial. Not hype-driven. Not "AI startup." More like a quiet, well-designed reading room. The product's core design principle is *calm over power*.
- **Competitive positioning**: Not a bookmark manager, not a knowledge base, not an AI summarizer. It's a personal space to finally *consume* the web you saved.

---

## Page structure

### Hero (full first viewport)

- **Headline**: "Turn the web into your personal podcast."
- **Subhead**: "Save any article. Listen to it with a natural voice. Understand it without breaking the flow."
- **Primary CTA**: "Get started — free" (or similar, low-friction)
- **Secondary CTA**: "See how it works" (scrolls to features or a brief demo flow)
- **Visual signal**: Avoid generic screenshots. Consider an abstract, typographic, or atmospheric visual that evokes *reading in a calm space* — or a minimalist illustration of the save → listen → understand flow. No stock photos. No laptop mockups with fake screens.

### How it works (3-step flow)

1. **Save** — Browser extension. One click. Article is extracted, cleaned from ads, popups, noise. Stored locally.
2. **Listen** — Modern TTS turns articles into audio. Natural voices, long-form, listening queue. Walk, commute, cook.
3. **Understand** — Tap any word or passage. Get a quick inline explanation. No tabs, no detours.

Each step should have a short, punchy description. Use iconography or minimal illustration, not screenshots.

### Why this, why now

Short section. Three points:
- "Pocket is gone. Millions of readers need a new home."
- "You save articles. You never read them. We fix that."
- "AI should help you read, not replace reading."

### Feature highlights

Not a feature grid. Pick the most emotionally resonant differentiators:

- **Offline-first**: Your articles, your device. No cloud required.
- **Bring your own voice**: Use your preferred TTS provider, or bring your own API key.
- **Calm by design**: No social feeds, no algorithms, no notifications. Just you and your articles.

### Social proof / trust

Placeholder section for future testimonials. For now, a single line like:
- "Built by readers, for readers."
- Or a minimal "Join X readers who..." counter (can start at zero, hidden if zero).

### Pricing teaser

Simple, honest:

- **Free**: Save and read articles. Always.
- **Pro** ($9/year): Unlimited listening, voice selection, smart explain. Less than a coffee per month.

Note the tone: not a hard sell. Just "here's what's free, here's what's worth a few dollars."

### FAQ

4-5 questions max. Calm, short answers:

- "Do I need an internet connection?" — Only to save. Reading and listening work offline.
- "Which TTS voices are supported?" — OpenAI, ElevenLabs, Gemini, local models. Or bring your own API key.
- "Is this a replacement for Pocket?" — Yes. Built for the people Pocket left behind.
- "Does it summarize articles?" — It can, but that's not the point. The point is helping you actually *read* them.
- "Is my data stored in the cloud?" — No. Articles are stored on your device. Offline-first, always.

### Footer

Minimal. Links to: Twitter/X, GitHub (if public), email, privacy policy. One-line tagline repeat. "Built with calm."

## Design direction

**Aesthetic**: Editorial, refined, warm minimalism. Think of a beautifully typeset book page, not a SaaS dashboard. Cream or off-white backgrounds, not pure white. Dark text with one restrained accent color (consider warm tones — amber, terracotta, soft gold — to evoke warmth and reading). Avoid blue, purple, or any "tech startup" palette.

**Typography**: This is a reading app. Typography IS the product signal. Use a distinctive, characterful serif or serif-sans pairing. The headline font should feel literary and modern — not corporate. Body text should be warm and highly readable. Avoid Inter, Roboto, Arial, system-ui, or any overused sans-serif.

**Whitespace**: Generous. The product is about calm. The page should feel like it breathes. No cramped sections, no dense feature grids.

**Motion**: Subtle. Gentle fade-ins on scroll. Maybe a soft wave or pulse on the "Listen" section to evoke audio. Nothing flashy.

**Responsive**: Must work beautifully on mobile. Most target users will first encounter the page on their phone during a commute.

**No external images**: No stock photos, no Unsplash, no placeholder images. Use typography, layout, color, and CSS effects (gradients, shadows, noise textures) to create atmosphere. If you need illustration, use simple inline SVG or CSS shapes.

---

## What to avoid

- Generic AI startup aesthetics (purple gradients, chat bubbles, robot imagery)
- SaaS dashboard vibes (card grids, data visualizations, progress bars)
- Hard-sell language ("10x your productivity", "supercharge your reading")
- Social proof that doesn't exist yet (fake logos, "trusted by Fortune 500")
- Feature overload — show restraint, match the product philosophy
- Stock photos of people smiling at laptops
